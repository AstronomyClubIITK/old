The 20th Century...

       Since then there have been many other achievements in astronomy some theoretical and others more practical but the basic tools had been laid out by 1700. As telescopes improved so did observations and in 1781 Herschel discovered Uranus. In 1846 Galle found Neptune and in 1930 Tombaugh discovered Pluto to complete the known solar system.

       It wasn't until late this century in the space age, that progress equivalent to that of the early Greeks was made. In 1957 the Russians launched the Sputnik craft placing man made objects into the space for the first time. By 1965, Yuri Gargarin had become the first man in space, Valentina Tereshkova the first woman in space and Leonov was the first to undertake space walk. In 1969 man set foot on another heavenly body for the first time when Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin landed on the moon . In December 1972 man left the moon for the last time - NASA was forced to abandon future missions due to finance.
       In the early to mid seventies the Pioneer and Voyager deep space probes were launched. Over the next 15 years they were to provide us with a wealth of information as they visited all the planets in the solar system except Pluto.
       Man's involvement in space had developed a new dimension with the American Skylab program and the Russian Soyuz spacecraft housing men and women in space for days on end. In 1981, the Shuttle program got underway and there have now been upwards of 70 Shuttle missions. The Russian program despite financial difficulties revolves around the Mir spacestation and has provided plenty of biological information on the effects of spending time in space. The next step is Freedom, an international space station. Possibly one day we will go back to moon and then onto Mars.
       The heavens are vast and we have explored so little! Yet what we do know is so beautiful and unimaginable that one always feels a sense of awe!!
