THE OBSERVATORY
       We need an observatory due to following reasons:-
1. The club members are now becoming interested in exploring the deep sky which requires sophisticated instruments. The photography of deep sky objects requires a good camera and a perfectly aligned telescope. This can be installed only in an observatory.
2. Assembling an 8" telescope is very tedious due to its heavy weight. We want to get it mounted permanently.
3. An observatory will definitely attract many more students and faculty members. It will be a big step ahead in our efforts to learn more about astronomy.
4. Last, but not the least, it will add to the long list of excellent facilities at IIT Kanpur .

THE RADIO TELESCOPE
       It all began in 1998 when The Astronomy club decided to build a radio telescope for observing loud radio sources such as emmision from our Solar System or from our Galactic Center. Intially we thought of building a telescope which is capable of "seeing" exceptionally loud sources such as VIR-A,CEN-A,CYG-A etc. But it does not work as expected.
       In Oct-1999 we had a talk with an emminent Radio Astronomer who suggested we should try to build a Radio Telescope using which one can do analysis also. Now our plan is to go for a telescope which can observe violent Solar Activities and other such sources in our solar system itself.
       We are going to install an antenna for observations. Our Radio Telescope has three parts
 
  • Antenna
     
  • Receiver
     
  • Read-Out Device
           We will use a 13 element yagi antenna with a cut off frequecy of 110MHz. It is significantly heavy and has one degree of freedom. When set, it will point towards the eclliptic and use its degree of freedom to scan the entire elliptic.
           We have decided to use a normal FM reciever avaliable in the market which is normally used in radios. The point here is that these recievers are build such that they can be used at 110MHz although they are best for working at 100MHz. As our work grows up we plan to built a reciever too, in collabration with Electronics Club, IIT-K.
           Our intial plan is to use a normal radio output as noise. But it will be too qualitative. To get some quantitative results we will be doing some experiments with this output using a Multimeter.
           According to professionals, this design gives satisfactory results.


    ADVANCES IN ASTROPHOTOGRAPHY
           Just now we are using a SLR Camera (Zenith) for Astrophotography. A Telephoto Lens of 400mm is attached to it to give better snaps of the clusters and nebulae. But the view field of the lens is only around 5-6 degrees which is insufficient for taking photographs of constellations or even metiorite showers etc.
           To overcome these drawbacks we are purchasing a Short Zoom to take the snaps of the constellations as a whole.
           A fish-eye lens of around 150 degrees viewfield is being purchased to enable us to take snaps of meteorites during a Meteorite Shower.
           After obtaining these accesories we hope to get better results.


    A CCD CAMERA INTERFACED WITH PC
           Now-a-days when everything is going online, why not astrophotography? The ages of     'piggy back' are long gone. Today everything is done with the aid of computer interfacing. We strongly feel the need of a CCD camera. This is the last step of our Four Phase Program. Definitely, this is going to be a very decisive step in the history of our Astronomy Club.
